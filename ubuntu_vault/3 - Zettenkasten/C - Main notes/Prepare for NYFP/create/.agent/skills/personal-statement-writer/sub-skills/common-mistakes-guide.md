# Common Mistakes Guide

## What NOT to Write in Personal Statements

### The Top 10 Mistakes

| #   | Mistake                   | Why It Fails        | Fix                                     |
| --- | ------------------------- | ------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **"Since childhood..."**  | Overused cliché     | Start with a specific recent moment     |
| 2   | **Listing achievements**  | Reads like a CV     | Tell stories about 2-3 key experiences  |
| 3   | **Being too humble**      | Misses the point    | Own your accomplishments confidently    |
| 4   | **Being too boastful**    | Seems arrogant      | Let achievements speak through context  |
| 5   | **Generic "why" reasons** | Shows no research   | Name specific professors, labs, courses |
| 6   | **Sob stories**           | Feels manipulative  | Frame challenges as growth, not pity    |
| 7   | **Using quotes**          | Wastes word count   | Use your own voice                      |
| 8   | **Writing an essay**      | Boring structure    | Use narrative/story arc                 |
| 9   | **Ignoring instructions** | Automatic rejection | Answer EXACTLY what they ask            |
| 10  | **Last-minute writing**   | Shows in quality    | Start 3+ weeks before deadline          |

### Cliché Phrases to Avoid

```
❌ "I have always been passionate about..."
❌ "In today's rapidly changing world..."
❌ "I believe education is the key to..."
❌ "This experience taught me the value of..."
❌ "I am a hard worker who..."
❌ "I want to make a difference..."
❌ "My journey began when..."
```

### The "Read Aloud" Test
Read your statement out loud. If it sounds like it could be written by anyone, it's too generic. Your statement should be so specific that no other applicant could have written it.
