# Harvard Paper Template

## Harvard Referencing Style

### Page Layout
- **Font**: Times New Roman or Arial, 12pt
- **Spacing**: Double or 1.5 spaced
- **Margins**: 1 inch (2.54cm) all sides
- **Page numbers**: Top right or bottom center

### Template Structure

```
                    Title of Paper

                    Author Name
                  Student ID (if applicable)
                    Department
                   University Name
                      Date

                     Abstract

[150-300 words summarizing the research]

Keywords: term1, term2, term3


1. Introduction

   Context and background (Author Year, p. X)...

2. Literature Review

   2.1 Theme One
   Previous studies indicate... (Smith 2020; Jones 2021).
   
   2.2 Theme Two

3. Methodology

4. Results

5. Discussion

6. Conclusion

Reference List

Author, A. (Year) Title of book. Edition. Place: Publisher.

Author, A. and Author, B. (Year) 'Title of article', Journal 
   Title, Volume(Issue), pp. X–Y.
```

### Harvard Citation Format

```
In-text:
  Single author:    (Author Year)  or  Author (Year)
  Two authors:      (Author and Author Year)
  Three+ authors:   (Author et al. Year)
  Direct quote:     (Author Year, p. X)
  Multiple works:   (Author Year; Author Year)
  Same author/year: (Author 2020a; Author 2020b)

Reference List:

Book:     Author, A.B. (Year) Title of Book. Edition. Place: 
          Publisher.

Journal:  Author, A.B. and Author, C.D. (Year) 'Title of article', 
          Journal Title, Volume(Issue), pp. X–Y. doi:xxxxx.

Chapter:  Author, A.B. (Year) 'Title of chapter', in Editor, E.F.
          (ed.) Title of Book. Place: Publisher, pp. X–Y.

Website:  Author/Organization (Year) Title of page. Available at: 
          URL (Accessed: Day Month Year).

Thesis:   Author, A.B. (Year) Title of thesis. Degree type. 
          University Name.
```

### Key Harvard Rules
1. Reference list alphabetical by author surname
2. Hanging indent for references
3. Book titles in *italics*
4. Article titles in 'single quotes'
5. Use "et al." for 3+ authors
6. Include "Available at:" and "Accessed:" for online sources
7. No "p." needed for paraphrasing, only direct quotes
