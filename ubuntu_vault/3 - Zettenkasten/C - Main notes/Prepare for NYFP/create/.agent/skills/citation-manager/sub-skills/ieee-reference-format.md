# IEEE Reference Format

## IEEE Citation Quick Reference

### In-Text Citations
References are numbered in square brackets in order of first appearance:
- Single: [1]
- Multiple: [1], [3]
- Range: [1]–[3]
- Combined: [1], [3], [5]–[7]

### Reference List Entries

```
Journal Article:
[1] A. B. Author and C. D. Author, "Title of article," Abbrev. 
    Title of Journal, vol. X, no. Y, pp. Z1–Z2, Abbrev. Month Year, 
    doi: 10.XXXX/XXXXX.

Conference Paper:
[2] A. Author, "Title of paper," in Proc. Full Name of Conf. 
    (Abbrev.), City, State/Country, Year, pp. X–Y.

Book:
[3] A. Author, Title of Book, Xth ed. City, State/Country: 
    Publisher, Year.

Book Chapter:
[4] A. Author, "Title of chapter," in Title of Book, Xth ed., 
    A. Editor, Ed. City: Publisher, Year, pp. X–Y.

Thesis:
[5] A. Author, "Title of thesis," M.S./Ph.D. thesis, Dept., 
    Univ., City, State/Country, Year.

Website:
[6] A. Author. "Title of page." Name. [Online]. Available: URL. 
    [Accessed: Mon. Day, Year].

Patent:
[7] A. Author, "Title of patent," Country Patent X XXX XXX, 
    Abbrev. Month Day, Year.
```

### IEEE Abbreviation Rules
- Journal names are abbreviated (IEEE Trans. → IEEE Transactions)
- Conference names abbreviated after first mention
- Month names abbreviated (Jan., Feb., Mar., etc.)
